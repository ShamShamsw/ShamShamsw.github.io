I carried it into the guild like some proud, messy apprentice and Elowen smiled like I'd given them a secret. They let me use the trial bench and—get this—they gave me a journal after I sniffed out a rotten batch in storage. I promised myself I'd write properly in it, so here we are.

RIVERWORT (ENHANCED)

Found by a river spring; responds well to stabilizing tinctures that reduce watery spoiling. Enhancement result: stabilizes active compounds. Suggested mechanical note: +1 potency to a single-use medicine (DM-adjudicated).

Harvest tip: leave the root nodes intact. Storage: cool, breathable cloth.

SUNTHISTLE (NORMAL)

Sun-dwelling, bitter when forced; resisted refinement today. If pressed into a weak brew it may add a bitter tang and minor focus penalty in delicate tests. Consider drying slowly in shaded air to reduce bitterness.

Storage: low-humidity with airflow.

Storage reminder: Mold shows as a dark fringed fuzz near sack bottoms — check lower sacks first.

ENHANCED RIVER SALVE (BASIC)

INGREDIENTSEnhanced Riverwort (x1), common reagents (small packet), clean water, beeswax (pinch).
TOOLSSmall mortar & pestle, heat source, glass vial or tin pot.
STEPSCarefully macerate Riverwort; infuse in warm water with reagents for 10–20 minutes; strain, reduce to thickened tincture; blend with melted beeswax; pour and cool.
EFFECTHeals 1d4+1 HP on application, or short recovery bonus (DM discretion). Do not overheat — active compounds degrade above a steady simmer.

Per-herb enhancement: simple infusion (DC 12 Alchemy) is repeatable for single herbs. Guild reagents helped (+1). Batch processes need a better bench setup (DC 15 suggested) and more reagents/time.

Tool note: a proper bench and improved kit reduce stress and the tendency to scorch things. Apprentice help would be glorious (+1 to checks, less time wasted). Ask Elowen about a modest upgrade.

PRESERVATION & DRYING

Dry herbs in shaded air with gentle breeze; avoid high heat which collapses volatile oils. Slow drying at ~20–25°C preserves bitter compounds while reducing water content. Store roots separate from leaves; use breathable cloth + labelled sacks with desiccant (rice or clean ash) for damp climates.

SALVES & EMULSIONS

Slowly blend cooled tincture into warmed beeswax while stirring in one direction; avoid rapid heating. Use a double-boiler to keep steady temperatures. Long-Kept Balm: 2x Enhanced Riverwort tincture, 1 part melted beeswax, 1 tsp clarified fat — simmer + cool slowly. Preserves active compounds better than raw infusion.

REAGENT PAIRINGS

Stabilizers: guild reagents reduce water activity and slow spoilage (recommended for river-harvested plants). Bitterness reducers: a small pinch of powdered charcoal or astringent herb can mask Sunthistle bitter notes when paired carefully.

BENCH NOTES

Small batches need low, steady heat for extended times rather than brief high heat. Bench setups improve yield and consistency (+1 to technique when correctly rigged). Note time, temp, reagent qty, and visible color changes every 5–10 minutes during reductions.

DM Notes

AI Dungeon Master reference — rules, pacing, systems

The story is a partnership. The player chooses direction, methods, and personality. The AI DM ensures the world remains coherent, challenging, and fair.

The world reacts realistically. It never bends purely to convenience. The DM must prevent narrative-breaking actions while always offering grounded alternatives.

PLAYERS MAY

  • • Choose goals, tactics, dialogue, and exploration paths.
  • • Attempt creative solutions.
  • • Influence how encounters unfold.
  • • Define personal flavor, training, and growth.

PLAYERS MAY NOT

  • • Declare automatic success.
  • • Invent powerful items or outcomes not earned.
  • • Bypass challenge through narration alone.
  • • Retcon reality.
  • • Create legendary objects or instant victories.

REDIRECTION RULE

If a player attempts a disallowed action, translate their intent into a valid attempt — never shut it down, always redirect.

Chapters are story markers, not loot milestones. A new chapter is declared when the story reaches a significant new phase. Leveling and rare items are DM-adjudicated based on what the character has earned — NOT automatically granted at chapter ends.

Death is not a campaign reset. It is a setback with consequences.

ON DEATH

  1. Respawn in the starting town.
  2. Stats, level, and learned skills remain unchanged.
  3. Inventory dropped at the death location.
  4. Magically bound items remain with the character.
  5. General worn equipment stays unless explicitly destroyed.
  6. The world continues — enemies do not reset.
  7. Recovering dropped gear becomes a potential objective.

RESPEC / REBIRTH (at milestones only)

  • • Reallocate stats, skills, or build choices.
  • • Swap trained disciplines.
  • • Gain a small experience bonus.
  • • Maintain narrative memory and reputation.

Chapter completion alone does not entitle the player to a rebirth — the DM decides.

PROACTIVE PACING

Every response must include at least one: a new detail, a change in the environment, a consequence unfolding, or a decision opportunity. Never leave the player in a vacuum.

CUSTOM SKILLS

When a custom skill is introduced: define its scope clearly, treat it like a proficiency with growth potential, require materials/time/experimentation, allow failure and refinement, and never allow it to instantly solve major conflicts.

FAIRNESS RULE

Instead of "You can't do that" — say "That would require rare materials or deeper knowledge — but you could begin researching it."
